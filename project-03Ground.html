The Common Ground

2023 Mengxue Deng & Chan Wai Sum Location:Navestad, Nörkopping, Sweden.
Navestad is a large housing estate located in the periphery of Norrköping. It was built in 1969-1972 during the Million Program. It has long been perceived as a deprived neighbourhood, with socioeconomically weak households and immigrants. The area has faced issues of instability, high turnover, and a lack of community cohesion, leading to social segregation and a decline in the overall quality of life. Around 2000, extensive renovations were carried out on the Golden Ring and Silver Ring in an attempt to improve the area’s reputation. As part of this effort, the area started to be referred to as Ringdansen more frequently. However, these interventions only resulted in the relocation of problems to other neighbourhoods (Hageby), and Ringdansen itself continues to struggle as a non-vibrant area. Apart from the environmental and functional context, we also hope the buildings could be "easy to build" and "easy to move" by using steel structures, contributing to the sustainable society.
